M.Tech Thesis

Cyclists’ Perceived Safety under Varying Traffic Conditions

Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru
Virtual bicycle simulator study in the Indian urban context
2024 – 2026

Research Objective

The study measures and models how cyclists’ perceived safety changes with bicycle-lane provision, traffic speed, and traffic volume, and relates subjective safety ratings to observed riding behaviour.

Research Design

  • Designed the experimental treatment structure and conducted pilot testing to improve repeatability and reduce bias.
  • Developed a structured questionnaire with Likert-scale perceived-safety items, demographic questions, and simulator-sickness checks.
  • Collected participant ratings immediately after each simulated traffic condition and linked responses to the correct treatment.
  • Cleaned simulator trajectory data and engineered speed, lateral distance, longitudinal position, acceleration, variability, and steering-related measures.

Modelling Approach

  • Structural Equation Modelling (SEM)
  • Ordered Logit and Ordered Probit models
  • Regression models for cyclist speed and lateral or kerb distance
  • Interaction analysis for bicycle lane, speed, and traffic volume

Planning Relevance

The work provides evidence for bicycle-lane provision, speed management, traffic calming, and cyclist-oriented urban road design in Indian cities.

Tools: R, Python, Unity, statistical and econometric modelling, experimental design
